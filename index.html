One ‚Äúmath snapshot‚Äù (optional on-page demo)

In many of my works, the adjacency (or coupling) structure of a network is encoded by a matrix \(A\), and spectral properties are studied via the eigenproblem \[ A\mathbf{v}=\lambda \mathbf{v}. \] Localization can be quantified (for example) by the inverse participation ratio \[ \mathrm{IPR}(\mathbf{v})=\sum_{i=1}^{N} |v_i|^4, \] where larger values indicate stronger concentration of eigenvector mass on fewer nodes.
