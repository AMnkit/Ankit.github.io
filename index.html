Projects

Selected research directions (readable summaries)

Parrondo-inspired routing in multiplex systems

Designing switching/alternating routing rules where two individually suboptimal choices can yield improved global performance when combined (Parrondo mechanism), and testing this under congestion and limited resources.

Related paper (PRR Letter)

Traffic management with limited resources

Studying congestion transitions and efficiency under constrained capacity, and developing strategies that re-balance load dynamically (e.g., switching rules) to reduce bottlenecks.

Related paper (Chaos, Solitons & Fractals)

Eigenvector localization & finite-size scaling

Quantifying how localization changes with system size and disorder strength, and identifying regimes where eigenvectors concentrate on small node sets versus delocalized behavior.

Hypergraphs: pairwise vs higher-order links

Comparing spectral localization under conventional edges versus higher-order interactions, and measuring how higher-order structure changes localization tendencies.
